Highlight Summary:

Finished Bachelor's degree in Electrical Engineering with honors and was awarded the "Best Bachelor's Thesis Award". The project focused on simulating and benchmarking In-Memory-Computing circuits to solve NP-Hard optimization problems using Hopfield Neural Networks.

My 6-month internship at IBM Research Zurich focused on creating a software stack to deploy state-of-the-art neural networks on the group's custom ASIC AI accelerators. This involved pipelining computational graphs, solving various optimization problems, calibrating chip hyper-parameters and creating a user-friendly programming experience. The software stack is still continued to be used internally and already led to new publications.

During my course-work, I also meaningfully extended the RTL of a complete System-on-Chip (SoC) and went through all the steps until tapeout, including synthesis, floorplanning, place, route, DRC and LVS checks. My design was one of the top 5 in the class and is therefore being taped-out. This gives me real digital-design and tapeout experience.
